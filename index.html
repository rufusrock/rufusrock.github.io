Experience

Research, teaching, and policy-facing work.

At Carnegie Mellon, I have worked as a teaching assistant across courses including Medical Ethics, Experience, Reason & Truth, and Ancient Philosophy, supporting more than 200 students through recitations, office hours, and written feedback.

In 2025, I held a research fellowship with the Lab of Symbolic & Educational Computing, studying the role of ceteris paribus clauses in causal modelling and the risks they pose for model generalization.

Before CMU, I worked with the UCL Institute for Innovation and Public Purpose on algorithmic attention rents, combining empirical work on Amazon search with broader questions about market power, platform governance, and digital regulation.
